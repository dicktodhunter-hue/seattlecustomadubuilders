Questions Seattle Homeowners Ask

How much does an ADU cost to build in Seattle?
In Seattle, expect $250,000-$400,000 for a detached ADU depending on size, finishes, and site conditions. Attached ADUs typically run $150,000-$250,000. Our builders provide detailed written estimates before any commitment.
How much rent can I charge for an ADU in Seattle?
Seattle ADUs typically rent for $1,800-$3,500/month depending on neighborhood, size, and finish quality. The average is $2,500/month — enough to cover most Seattle mortgage payments.
Do I need to live on the property to build or rent an ADU?
No — as of July 2025, Seattle eliminated the owner-occupancy requirement entirely. You can build, rent, or even move out completely without any impact on your ADU's legal status.
How long does the permit process take?
Under Seattle's 2025 rules, permits using pre-approved plans can be issued in as little as 2-6 weeks. Our builders know exactly which plan sets qualify and how to navigate the process efficiently.
What about tiny homes and land in the Seattle area?
For those dreaming of their own small home on their own land — fill out our assessment form and tell us your story. We are building resources specifically for this path and you'll be among the first to know when we launch.
