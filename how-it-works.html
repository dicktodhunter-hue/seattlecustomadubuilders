The four steps from idea to income.

Every Seattle ADU and tiny home project follows this proven path. Here's what to expect at each stage.

Step 01
Plan — Free Feasibility Review
Before you spend a dollar, we analyze your specific lot — its GIS data, zoning overlays, setbacks, and utility maps. We show you exactly what's possible, what it costs, and what it will earn. No obligation, no pressure. Just clear answers.
Timeline: 1–2 weeks
Step 02
Design — Your ADU Takes Shape
Working with a licensed Seattle architect, your ADU is designed to maximize rental value, natural light, privacy, and neighborhood compatibility. Seattle's pre-approved DADU designs can dramatically speed up this phase and reduce architectural fees.
Timeline: 4–8 weeks
Step 03
Permit — City Approval Made Simple
Your builder manages the entire Seattle permitting process — submitting plans, responding to city comments, and tracking approvals. Using Seattle's ADUniverse pre-approved designs, permits can be issued in as little as 2–6 weeks. Standard permits typically take 4–8 weeks under 2025 rules.
Timeline: 2–8 weeks
Step 04
Build — From Ground to Keys
Construction begins once permits are issued. A typical Seattle DADU takes 6–12 months to complete depending on size and complexity. Garage conversions and basement ADUs are often faster — 4–8 months. Your builder keeps you informed at every milestone, from foundation to final inspection.
Timeline: 4–12 months

What makes a great Seattle ADU builder?

📋
Verified local portfolio
Look for completed Seattle ADU and DADU projects — not just renderings. Ask for photos, floor plans, and references from recent King County builds.
🏛️
Seattle permit expertise
Your builder should know Seattle's ADUniverse program, the 2025 HB 1337 changes, and how to navigate SDCI reviews without costly delays.
💰
Transparent pricing
A trustworthy builder provides clear cost breakdowns before you sign anything. Seattle DADUs typically run $250,000–$400,000 — no surprises.
📅
Realistic timelines
Beware of builders promising unusually fast builds. A quality Seattle DADU takes time done right. Ask for a detailed project schedule upfront.
⚡
Washington State licensed
Verify your builder holds a current Washington State Contractor License from L&I, plus appropriate liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage.
🤝
Feasibility first policy
The best Seattle ADU builders always start with a free feasibility review before asking you to sign anything. That's the standard we hold our builders to.
